# Company And Master Data

Before billing starts, complete company details, common records, contacts, and products. Good setup makes daily entry faster and reduces correction work.

![Setup order](/img/docs/setup-order.svg)

## Company

Keep these details updated:

- Legal name and display name.
- GSTIN and state.
- Address and contact details.
- Bank details.
- Logo and print preferences.

These details appear on invoices, quotations, reports, and printed documents.

## Common Records

Common records are reusable values used inside forms. Set them before entering daily documents.

Typical common records:

- HSN codes.
- Taxes and rates.
- Units.
- Countries, states, districts, cities, and pincodes.
- Payment terms.
- Product categories, groups, brands, colours, and sizes.

Add a common record once, then select it from lookup boxes wherever needed.

## Contacts

Create contacts for customers, suppliers, and other parties. A good contact record should include:

- Name.
- Mobile or email when available.
- GSTIN if registered.
- Billing and shipping address.
- Contact type such as customer or supplier.

Use the existing contact if it already exists. Avoid creating the same party twice with slightly different spelling.

## Products

Create products with:

- Product name.
- HSN code.
- Unit.
- Tax rate.
- Category/group where useful.
- Sale or purchase rate when standard rates are used.

When products are complete, sales and purchase entries can fill HSN, unit, and tax quickly.

## Recommended Setup Order

1. Company profile.
2. Accounting year and default company.
3. Common records.
4. Contacts.
5. Products.
6. Document numbering.
7. Start daily entries.
