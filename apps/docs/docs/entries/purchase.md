# Purchase Entry

Use Purchase Entry to record a supplier bill, input GST, expense or stock value, and the amount payable to the supplier.

![New Purchase entry screen](/img/docs/entries/purchase-entry.png)

## Before You Start

- Select the correct company and accounting year.
- Create the supplier and its addresses.
- Keep the original supplier bill number and bill date ready.
- Create products with HSN code, unit, and GST rate.
- Confirm the Purchase Ledger and numbering settings.

## Create a Purchase

1. Open **Billing > Entries > Purchase**.
2. Select **New Purchase**.
3. Search and select the **Supplier name**.
4. Confirm the suggested **Entry no**, or enter an unused manual number.
5. Confirm the **Entry date**.
6. Enter the supplier's exact **Supplier bill no** and **Supplier bill date**.
7. Select the required **Purchase Ledger**.
8. Select **CGST-SGST** or **IGST** according to the supplier and place of supply.
9. Enter the **Work Order no** when applicable.

## Add Items

1. Search and select the product.
2. Create the product from the lookup if it does not exist.
3. Confirm HSN code, unit, and GST rate populated from the Product master.
4. Enter PO/DC references, description, quantity, and purchase price.
5. Select **Add**.
6. Repeat for all bill lines.
7. Compare taxable value, GST, round off, and grand total with the supplier bill.

## Address and Other Tabs

- **Address:** Select or create billing/shipping addresses for the supplier.
- **E-way:** Record transport, vehicle, and E-way details when required.
- **E-invoice:** Store compliance details when the workflow is enabled.
- **Terms:** Record internal notes and purchase terms.

## Save and Verify

1. Select **Save** or **Save & Print**.
2. Reopen the purchase and compare it with the original supplier bill.
3. Verify supplier bill number, bill date, HSN, GST rates, quantities, and totals.
4. Check the Supplier Statement after payments are allocated.

## Common Mistakes

- Entering your internal Entry no as the Supplier bill no.
- Recording the same supplier bill twice.
- Using the wrong purchase tax type.
- Selecting an incorrect Purchase Ledger.
- Adding a product as text without creating or selecting its Product master.
