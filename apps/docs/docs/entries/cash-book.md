# Cash Book Entry

Use Cash Book for direct cash-ledger movements that are not entered through the customer Receipt or supplier Payment workflow.

![New Cash Book entry screen](/img/docs/entries/cash-book-entry.png)

## Choose the Correct Entry

- Use **Receipt** for customer money received and invoice allocation.
- Use **Payment** for supplier money paid and bill allocation.
- Use **Cash Book** for other cash income, expenses, transfers, or adjustments.

## Create a Cash Book Entry

1. Open **Billing > Accounts > Cash Book**.
2. Select **New Cash Book**.
3. Confirm the **Cash ledger**.
4. Select the opposite **Ledger** affected by the transaction.
5. Confirm the **Voucher no** and **Date**.
6. Enter optional **Particulars**.
7. Enter the **Amount**.
8. Choose **Received** when cash increases or **Paid** when cash decreases.
9. Select the required **Status**.
10. Enter the Work Order number and narration when applicable.
11. Open **Notes** for additional internal information.
12. Save the entry.

## Verify the Posting

1. Confirm the entry appears in Cash Book.
2. Check the direction and selected opposite ledger.
3. Verify the cash balance and Day Book movement.

## Common Mistakes

- Using Paid when cash was received.
- Selecting the cash ledger again as the opposite ledger.
- Entering customer/supplier settlements here instead of using Receipt/Payment allocations.
- Posting a bank transaction through Cash Book.
