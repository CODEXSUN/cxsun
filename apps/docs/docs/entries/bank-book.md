# Bank Book Entry

Use Bank Book for direct bank-ledger movements that are not entered through customer Receipt or supplier Payment allocation.

![New Bank Book entry screen](/img/docs/entries/bank-book-entry.png)

## Choose the Correct Entry

- Use **Receipt** for customer collections.
- Use **Payment** for supplier payments.
- Use **Bank Book** for bank charges, interest, direct deposits, direct debits, and other bank-ledger adjustments.

## Create a Bank Book Entry

1. Open **Billing > Accounts > Bank Book**.
2. Select **New Bank Book**.
3. Confirm the **Bank ledger**.
4. Select the opposite **Ledger** affected by the transaction.
5. Confirm the **Voucher no** and **Date**.
6. Enter optional **Particulars**.
7. Enter the **Amount**.
8. Choose **Received** when the bank balance increases or **Paid** when it decreases.
9. Select the required **Status**.
10. Enter the Work Order number and narration when applicable.
11. Open **Notes** for bank reference, cheque, UTR, or internal details.
12. Save the entry.

## Verify the Posting

1. Confirm the transaction appears in Bank Book.
2. Verify the direction and opposite ledger.
3. Compare the entry with the bank statement.
4. Check Day Book and ledger balances.

## Common Mistakes

- Using Received/Paid in the wrong direction.
- Selecting the bank ledger as both sides of the entry.
- Entering customer or supplier settlements without Receipt/Payment allocation.
- Omitting bank reference details needed for reconciliation.
